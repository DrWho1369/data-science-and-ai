üìå What is a Probability Distribution?

A probability distribution describes how probabilities are assigned to different values of a random variable.

  • A random variable (X) is a function that assigns numerical values to each outcome of a random experiment.
  • There are two types of probability distributions:
    • Discrete Probability Distributions (e.g., number of customer complaints per day).
    • Continuous Probability Distributions (e.g., time taken to complete a task).

üîπ Example:

The outcome of flipping a fair coin 3 times gives the sample space:

S = {HHH, HHT, HTH, THH, HTT, THT, TTH, TTT}

The random variable (X) could represent the number of heads. The probability distribution would assign probabilities to each X value (0,1,2,3 heads).

üìå Binomial Distribution

The binomial distribution models the number of successes in n independent Bernoulli trials.

1Ô∏è‚É£ Characteristics of a Binomial Experiment

  • Fixed number of trials (n).
  • Each trial has only two possible outcomes (Success or Failure).
  • The probability of success (p) remains constant.
  • Trials are independent.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Binomial Probability Formula

P(X = k) = (n choose k) p^k (1 - p)^(n - k)
  • n = number of trials
  • k = number of successes
  • p = probability of success
  • (n choose k) = "n choose k" (binomial coefficient)

üîπ Example: Probability of getting 2 heads in 4 coin flips

P(X=2) = (4 choose 2) (0.5)^2 (0.5)^2 = 6 √ó 0.0625 = 0.375

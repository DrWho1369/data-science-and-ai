üìå What is Hypothesis Testing?

Hypothesis testing is a statistical method used to test assumptions about a population. It helps us determine if our sample data provides enough evidence to support or reject a claim.

üîπ Example in Business:

A company claims its customer satisfaction score is above 80%. We can test this assumption using hypothesis testing.

üìå Steps in Hypothesis Testing

  1. State the Hypothesis
    • The Null Hypothesis (H‚ÇÄ): Assumes no effect or no difference. (e.g., The mean is 80%)
    • The Alternative Hypothesis (H‚ÇÅ): States what we seek to prove. (e.g., The mean is greater than 80%).
  2. Specify the Decision Rule
    • Determine the significance level (Œ±), typically 0.05 or 0.01.
    • Decide on a one-tailed or two-tailed test.
  3. Collect Data & Calculate Test Statistic
    • Compute Z-score or T-score to compare the sample data with the null hypothesis.
  4. Make a Decision
    • If test statistic falls in the rejection region, reject H‚ÇÄ.
    • Otherwise, fail to reject H‚ÇÄ.
  5. Take Action
    • Based on the results, make a business or research decision.
